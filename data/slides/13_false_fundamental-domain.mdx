# Fundamental domain
<br />

<Theorem title="fundamental domain">
  Let $X$ be a space on which a group $G$ acts. We say that a closed subset $Y\subseteq X$ is the **fundamental domain** of this action if 
  - sets $gY$ cover $X$
  - $gY \cap Y=\emptyset$ when $g\neq 1$
</Theorem>

In other words: for every orbit $Gx$ we take exactly one element and put it in $Y$.

We will denote the fundamental domain of $G$ acting on $X$ by $X\mod G$.
<br />

### Example

Consider the action of $\mathbb{Z}$ on the real line $\mathbb{R}$ by translations. The circle $S^1$ is the fundamental domain of this action.
