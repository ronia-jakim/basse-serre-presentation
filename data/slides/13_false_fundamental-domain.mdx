# Fundamental domain
<br />

<Theorem title="fundamental domain">
  Let $X$ be a space on which a group $G$ acts. We say that a closed subset $Y\subseteq X$ is the **fundamental domain** of this action if 
  - sets $gY$ cover $X$
  - $gY \cap Y=\emptyset$ when $g\neq 1$
</Theorem>

In other words: for every orbit $Gx$ we take exactly one element and put it in $Y$.

### Example

Consider the action of $\mathbb{Z}$ on the real line $\mathbb{R}$ by translations. The circle $S^1$ is the fundamental domain of this action.

### Example 

$PSL(2,\mathbb{Z})$ acts on the hyperbolic plane, identified with the upper half plane $H=\{z\in\mathbb{C}\;:\;Re(z)\geq0\}$. 

The fundamental domain of this action is $F=\{z\in H\;:\;|z|>1,\;|Re(z)|<\frac{1}{2}\}$

<br />

Why? Take any point $z\in H$. WLOG $Im(z)>1$ - otherwise "inversions" and translations will help.

Once we are in $Im(z)>1$ simply apply $\begin{bmatrix}1 & \pm 1 \\ 0 & 1\end{bmatrix}$ untill in $F$.
