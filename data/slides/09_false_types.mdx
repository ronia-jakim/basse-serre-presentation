# Those are the ends
<div style={{ textAlign: 'right', fontSize: 12, color: 'grey' }}>
*beautiful friends \\ those are the ends \\ my only friends, the ends*
</div>
<br />

We say that a subgraph $P\subseteq T$ is a **ray** if it is isometric with $[0,\infty)$.
<br />
<div style={{ textAlign: 'center' }}>
$P\sim P'$ iff $P\cap P'$ is an infinite subgraph
</div>
<br />

<Theorem title="ends of a tree">
  The set of equivalence classes of rays in a tree is called its **boundary**, $\partial T$.
</Theorem>

Example:
- $\mathbb{R}_+$ as a tree has one boundary component
- $\mathbb{R}$ as an infinite tree has two boundary components
- Cayley graph of $F_2$ has infinitely many boundary components (it do be $\{0,1\}^\mathbb{N}$)

There are no other options.
