# Fundamental domain

Let $G$ be a group acting on a nonempty tree $X$. 
<br />

In this case the **fundamental domain** $X\mod G$ can be more precisely defined as a subgraph $T\subseteq X$ such that $T\to G\backslash X$ is an isomorphism.

<Theorem title="Twierdzoniunko">
A fundamental domain $X\mod G$ exists iff $G\backslash X$ is a tree. 
</Theorem>

<ConfettiTrigger />
**Proof**

$\impliedby$ 

goes from the definition above.

$\implies$ 

- let $T=X\mod G$
<div style={{ height: '-2px' }} />

- $G\backslash X$ is a tree
<div style={{ height: '-2px' }} />

- $G\backslash X$ is connected (a tree) and nonempty (because $X$ was nonempty)
<div style={{ height: '-2px' }} />

- $T\cong G\backslash X$ is a connected subgraph of a tree $\implies$ $T=$🌳
