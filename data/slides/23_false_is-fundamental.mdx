# Amalgams and fundamental domains

We will now work tireresly to prove the following theorem.

<Theorem title="Twierdzoneczko me słoneczko">
  Let $(G, T)$ be a tree of groups. There is a graph $X$ containing $T$ and an action of $G_T=\varinjlim(G,T)$ on $X$ which is characterized (up to isomorphism) by the following property:
  - $T$ is the fundamental domain $X\mod G$
  - $(\forall\;P\in V(T))\;Stab(P)_{G_T}=G_P$ (repeat for edges)
  - $X$ is a tree
</Theorem>

**We say that $X$ as in the theorem are assosiated with $(G, T)$**

<ConfettiTrigger />
**Proof** 

part 1: construct a candidate for $X$

$$
V(X):=\bigsqcup_{P\in V(T)} G_TP
$$ 
which are isomorphic to $G_T/G_P$ and
$$
E(X):=\bigsqcup_{\gamma\in E(T)} G_T\gamma=\bigsqcup_{g\in G_T}\{g\cdot \gamma=(gP, gQ)\;:\;\gamma=(P, Q)\in E(T)\}
$$
which are isomorphic to $G_T/G_\gamma$.
<div style={{ height: '15px' }} />

The inclusions: $G_\gamma\to G_P$ and $G_{\overline{\gamma}}\to G_Q$ for $\gamma=(P, Q)$ are defined for representatives of each orbit the same way as in $(G,T)$.
<div style={{ height: '15px' }} />


What is clear:
1. $X$ is a graph.
<div style={{ height: '-2px' }} />
2. The group $G_T$ acts on $X$ by left translations.
