# Fundamental group of a graph of groups

<br />

$$
F(G, Y)=\langle G_P, \gamma\;:\;P\in V(Y), \gamma\in E(Y)\;|\; \overline{\gamma}=\gamma^{-1},\gamma \sigma_\gamma(a)\gamma^{-1}=\sigma_{\overline{\gamma}}(a)\;:\;a\in G_\gamma\rangle
$$
if $c=(P_0, P_1,...,P_n)$ is a path in $Y$ with edges $\gamma_1,...,\gamma_n$ and $\mu=(r_0,r_1,...,r_n)$ are elements of $G_{i}$ then 
$$
|c, \mu|=r_0\gamma_1r_1\gamma_2r_2...\gamma_nr_n\in F(G,Y)
$$

Two ways of defining the fundamental group.
<br />

## via maximal tree $T$

$$
\pi_1(G, Y, T)=F(G, Y)/\langle \gamma\;:\;\gamma\in E(T) \rangle
$$
<br />

## as loops

$$
\pi_1(G, Y, P_0)=\{ |c, \mu|\in F(G, Y)\;:\;\text{both ends of }c\text{ are }P_0 \}
$$

<Theorem title="Propozycja nie do odrzucenia">
  The canonical projection $p:F(G, Y)\to \pi_1(G, Y, T)$ induces an isomrphism $\pi_1(G, Y, P_0)\to \pi_1(G, Y, T)$
</Theorem>

**Proof** 

define $f$ that is the inverse of $p$

$f(x)=\gamma_Px\gamma_P^{-1}$ if $x\in G_P$
