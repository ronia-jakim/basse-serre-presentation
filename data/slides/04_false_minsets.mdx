# Tree isometries
<br />

<Theorem title="Twierdzonko">
  Let $T$ be a tree and $\phi:T\to T$ its isometry. Then
  - if $\phi$ is elliptic then $Min(\phi)=Fix(\phi)$ is a subtree or the middle of some edge
  - if $\phi$ is hyperbolic then $Min(\phi)$ is a line $L\subseteq T$.
</Theorem>

The line $L$ in hyperbolic isometry is called *axis* of $\phi$ and we will denote it as $Axis(\phi)$.
<br />

### Examples

Consider the $k$-regular tree $T_k$.
- elliptic
  - group: $S_k$
  - chose a vertex $v\in T_k$
  - $\sigma\in S_k$ acts on $T_k$ by permuting the neighbours of $v$.

- hyperbolic: 
  - group: $\mathbb{Z}$
  - chose a line $L=\{..., v_{-1}, v_0, v_1, ...\}$ in $T_k$ 
  - define the action of $\mathbb{Z}$ on $T_k$ as follows: the generator $1\in \mathbb{Z}$ moves $v_i$ to $v_{i+1}$
