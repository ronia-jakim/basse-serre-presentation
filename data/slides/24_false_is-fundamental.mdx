# Amalgams and fundamental domains

<Theorem title="Twierdzoneczko me słoneczko" defaultOpen={false} >
  Let $(G, T)$ be a tree of groups. There is a graph $X$ containing $T$ and an action of $G_T=\varinjlim(G,T)$ on $X$ which is characterized (up to isomorphism) by the following property:
  - $T$ is the fundamental domain $X\mod G$
  - $(\forall\;P\in V(T))\;Stab(P)_{G_T}=G_P$ (repeat for edges)
  - $X$ is a tree
</Theorem>

**Proof** 

part 2: inductively proving $X$ is a tree

- Represent $T$ as a direct limit of a direct system of increasing finite subtrees.
<div style={{ height: '-2px' }} />
- $G_T=\varinjlim (G, T)$
<div style={{ height: '-2px' }} />
- $X$ can be represented as a direct limit of graphs in the same way $G_T$ is a direct limit (but put orbits $G_TP$ instead of $G_P$ on the diagram)

This reduces the problem to $T$ finite.
<br />

Induction on $n=|V(T)|$.
- $n=1$ then $X=T$
<div style={{ height: '-2px' }} />
- $n>1$
  - $\gamma\in E(T)$ such that $\gamma$ ends in a leaf $P\in T$, $T'=T-P$
  - $G_T=G_{T'}\ast_{G_\gamma}G_P$
  - $X'=G_{T'}T'$ is a subgraph of $X$ **inductive hypothesis** $X'$ is a tree
  - $gX'$ for $g\in G_T/G_{T'}$ are pariwise disjoint ($gX'=hX'\implies g^{-1}h\in G_{T'}\implies gX'\cap hX'\cap V(T')=\bigsqcup G_{T'}T'\neq \emptyset$)
  - $\overline{X}=X$ with $gX'$ contracted to points is a tree since it has fundamental domain $T/T'$ is the edge $\gamma$ with initial vertex being $T'$ compressed to a point and terminal vertex being $P$.
<Theorem title="Twierdzoniunko" defaultOpen={false}>
A fundamental domain $X\mod G$ exists iff $G\backslash X$ is a tree. 
</Theorem>
