# Universal cover

- Let $G$ act on a connected graph $X$ without edge inversions. 
- Let $Y=G\backslash X$ and $T$ be its maximal tree. 
- We can construct a graph of groups $(G, Y)$ such $\pi_1(G, Y, T)\cong G$.
- Let $\overline{X}$ be the universal cover of $Y$.

<Theorem title="Stwierdzenie">
  The following are equivalent
  1. $X$ is a tree
  2. $\psi:\widetilde{X}\to X$ is an isomorphism
  3. $\phi:\pi_1(G, Y, T)\to G$ is an isomorphism
</Theorem>

<ConfettiTrigger />
**Proof** 

$1\implies 2$ & $3\implies 1$

<Theorem title="Twierdzoneczko me słoneczko" defaultOpen={false} >
  The following are equivalent:
  1. $X$ - tree
  2. $\widetilde{X}\to X$ isomorphism
  3. $G_T\to G$ isomorphism
</Theorem>

$2\implies 1$

because universal cover of $Y$ is a tree

$2\implies 3$

$N=\ker\phi=\ker(\pi_1(G, Y, T)\supseteq G_P\mapsto Stab(jP)\subseteq  G)$

$P\in V(Y)$ then $N\cap G_{\widetilde{P}}=\{1\}$

$n\in N-\{1\}$ then $\widetilde{P}\neq n\widetilde{P}$ in $\widetilde{X}$ but $\psi(n\widetilde{P})=\phi(n)jP=jP=\psi(\widetilde{P})$
